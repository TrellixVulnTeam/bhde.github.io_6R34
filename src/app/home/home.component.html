Basinio da Parma’s Hesperis – Digital Born Critical Edition of Books 1 and 2

Although the Neo-Latin poet Basinio da Parma (1425–1457) died at an early age, his life was very productive. Especially the last years, which he spent in Rimini under the patronage of Sigismondo Malatesta, were characterized by a large literary output. Basinio’s poems are exemplary works of 15th-century Neo-Latin literature, and cover various canonical genres. His opus magnum is the epic poem Hesperis, in which he celebrates the war exploits of his patron Sigismondo Malatesta. Its poetic quality and historical significance make it all the more surprising that we still have to rely on the 1794 edition by Lorenzo Drudi.

In 2018, the Ludwig Boltzmann Institute for Neo-Latin Studies launched a project funded by the Austrian Fonds zur Förderung der wissenschaftlichen Forschung (FWF). Our research team has approached the text of the Hesperis in several ways: In addition to the critical edition, for which Dr. Anna Chisena was primarily responsible, there is an English translation by Simon Smets. This is complemented by Nikolaus Hölzl’s commentary on the first two books. All these elements were integrated in the digital presentation developed by Marco Petolicchio.

This innovative digital format allows a user to view the critical text of Books 1 and 2 of the Hesperis with a critical apparatus and an apparatus indicating similar passages in ancient Latin literature. The reader can choose to consult the English translation and the commentary in parallel.

We hope you enjoy working with this digital edition!
